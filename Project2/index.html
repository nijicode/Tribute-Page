Ada Lovelace

The World's First Computer Programmer

Ada Lovelace's Biography

  • 1815 - Born in London, England
  • 1832 - When she was seventeen, her mathematical abilities began to emerge, and her interest in mathematics dominated the majority of her adult life.
  • 1833 - Lovelace first met Charles Babbage through their mutual friend Mary Somerville. Later that month, Babbage invited Lovelace to see the prototype for his difference engine.She became fascinated with the machine and used her relationship with Somerville to visit Babbage as often as she could.
  • 1840 - The mathematician Augustus De Morgan extended her "much help in her mathematical studies" including study of advanced calculus topics including the "numbers of Bernoulli" (that formed her celebrated algorithm for Babbage's Analytical Engine).
  • 1843 - She published edition of Taylor's Scientific Memoirs under the initialism AAL. Ada Lovelace's notes were labelled alphabetically from A to G. In Note G, she describes an algorithm for the Analytical Engine to compute Bernoulli numbers. It is considered to be the first published algorithm ever specifically tailored for implementation on a computer, and Ada Lovelace has often been cited as the first computer programmer for this reason.
  • 1843 - Babbage was impressed by Lovelace's intellect and analytic skills. He called her "The Enchantress of Number".
  • 1852 - Lovelace died at the age of 36 from uterine cancer.
  • 1953 - More than a century after her death, Ada Lovelace's notes on Babbage's Analytical Engine were republished as an appendix to B. V. Bowden's Faster than Thought: A Symposium on Digital Computing Machines.The engine has now been recognised as an early model for a computer and her notes as a description of a computer and software.
